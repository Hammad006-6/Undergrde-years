# Undergrde-years
This is my first GitHub repository.
Your first semester at university is often a life-changing phase filled with a mix of excitement, independence, and responsibility. In the beginning, everything feels new—your campus, classrooms, teachers, and especially the freedom to manage your own schedule. Regular classes become an important part of your daily routine, and unlike school, you quickly realize that staying consistent is your own responsibility. Some lectures feel interesting and engaging, while others test your patience, but each one contributes to building your academic foundation.

As you start settling in, you begin forming friendships with classmates who soon become your support system. Group discussions, shared notes, and even casual hangouts between classes make university life more enjoyable. One of the most exciting parts of the semester is sports week, where the usual academic environment shifts into a vibrant, energetic atmosphere. Students participate in games like cricket, football, and athletics, while others cheer from the sidelines. It’s a time full of enjoyment, team spirit, and a refreshing break from studies.

Alongside these activities, quizzes begin to appear in your schedule. These are usually short but important assessments that keep you engaged with your coursework. They encourage regular study habits and help you understand where you stand academically. Soon after, midterm exams arrive, often bringing the first real sense of pressure. Students spend long hours preparing, revising lectures, and sometimes experiencing stress, but mids also teach you time management and discipline.

After midterms, there’s usually a short period where things feel a bit relaxed. Students become more confident and comfortable with university life. However, this phase doesn’t last long because final exams gradually approach. The workload increases, assignments pile up, and revision becomes more intense. Final exams are more comprehensive and require a deeper understanding of all the topics covered throughout the semester.

By the end of the semester, you realize how much you’ve grown—not just academically, but personally as well. You learn how to balance studies and enjoyment, handle pressure, and adapt to a new environment. The combination of regular classes, fun events like sports week, continuous quizzes, and the challenges of mid and final exams makes the first semester a memorable journey that shapes your entire university experience.
